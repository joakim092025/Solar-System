Inner Planets

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun. It is small and has virtually no atmosphere. Its surface is covered with craters, similar to Earth's Moon.

Venus

Venus is often called Earth's twin because of its similar size. It has a thick atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide, which creates an extreme greenhouse effect. Its surface is hot enough to melt lead.

Earth

Earth is the only planet known to support life. It has liquid water on the surface and a stable atmosphere. Earth has one natural satellite, the Moon.

Mars

Mars is known as the Red Planet due to the iron oxide (rust) on its surface. It has polar ice caps and signs that liquid water once flowed there. Mars has two small moons: Phobos and Deimos.

Outer Planets

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system. It has a thick atmosphere with massive storms, including the Great Red Spot. Jupiter has more than 90 moons, including Ganymede, the largest moon in the solar system.

Saturn

Saturn is well known for its stunning ring system. It is the second-largest planet and has many moons, including Titan. Saturn is composed mostly of hydrogen and helium.

Uranus

Uranus has a pale blue-green color due to methane gas in its atmosphere. It rotates on its side, with an axis nearly horizontal to its orbit. Uranus is extremely cold and has rings as well as at least 27 moons.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and has a deep blue color. It experiences very strong winds and storms. Neptune has several moons, including Triton.
